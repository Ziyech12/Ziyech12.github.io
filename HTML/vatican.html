Place

            The Vatican City is the Holy See's independaent city state, an enclave within Rome, Italy. Also knows as The Vatican, The city state's temporal, diplomatic, and spiritual independence.


How to get there?

There are 3 main transportation options to get from Fiumicino Airport to the Vatican City. The first and most common option is by taxi. The second option, and the most preferred public transportation option, is the Leonardo Express train, which requires a connecting stop at Roma Trastevere Railway Station. The third, and least common option, is to take the bus from Fiumicino Airport to Roma Termini and then taking a connecting bus to S. Pietro station near the Vatican City. Taking a Fiumicino airport taxi costs between €55 and €60, and the total trip takes around 35 minutes, in normal traffic conditions. The overall train option costs a total of €14 and takes 1 hour and 15 minutes, including the waiting time at Roma Trastevere Railway Station. Lastly, there are three bus options from Fiumicino Airport to Roma termini and two corresponding bus choices from Termini station to the Vatican City. The total bus trip takes approximately 1 hour and 15 minutes, including the waiting times, and the combined price comes to a total of €7.50.
